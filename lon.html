About Zamboanga City - Philippines

Welcome to Zamboanga City, the vibrant cultural melting pot of the Philippines where East meets West. Known as "Asia's Latin City," Zamboanga boasts a unique blend of Spanish, Malay, and indigenous influences. From the colorful vintas to the famous pink sand beach of Sta. Cruz Island, our city offers unforgettable experiences that celebrate our rich heritage and natural beauty. Zamboanga City is also known for its Chavacano language, a Spanish-based creole, and its delicious cuisine featuring curacha, knickerbocker, and other local delicacies.

Must-Visit Attractions in Zamboanga

Sta. Cruz Island

Famous for its pink sand beach caused by crushed red coral mixed with white sand, this protected marine sanctuary offers crystal-clear waters perfect for snorkeling.

Fort Pilar

This 17th-century military defense fortress is now a national cultural treasure and museum showcasing Zamboanga's rich history and heritage.

Paseo del Mar

The city's premier waterfront park with stunning sunset views, cultural performances, and a perfect place to try local street food.

Yakan Weaving Village

Visit this village to see the intricate art of Yakan weaving and purchase authentic handwoven fabrics known for their vibrant colors and geometric patterns.

Merloquet Falls

A beautiful two-tiered waterfall located in the lush forests of Sibulao, perfect for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

Great Santa Cruz Island

Known for its giant clams and excellent snorkeling spots with diverse marine life, this island is a paradise for underwater enthusiasts.

Upcoming Events in Zamboanga

Hermosa Festival

October 1-12, 2025

The Zamboanga Hermosa Festival, also known as Fiesta Pilar, is a month-long celebration in honor of Our Lady of the Pillar, the city's patron saint. The festival features street dancing, cultural shows, and the famous Regatta de Zamboanga with colorful vinta sailboats.

Chavacano Cultural Night

June 23, 2025

A celebration of Zamboanga's unique Chavacano language and culture featuring traditional dances, music, and theatrical performances showcasing the city's Hispanic heritage.

Zamboanga Food Festival

August 15-18, 2025

A culinary event showcasing Zamboanga's unique cuisine including curacha (deep-sea crab), knickerbocker (fruit dessert), and other local delicacies. Cooking demonstrations and food competitions included.
